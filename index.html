What is PCC LEAH Friday coop?

PCC LEAH Friday co-op is a cooperation of parents working together to enrich their individual homeschools with different age-appropriate classes. A Co-op Board made of chapter members oversees the co-op. Participation is optional. There are two sessions planned for this year: fall and winter/spring.

Friday Co-op is just one of the many things PCC LEAH offers to support, encourage and enrich member’s homeschools. Besides Friday co-op, there are support meetings, Care and Share Nights, Men’s Breakfast, prayer support, test groups, fieldtrips, etc. These areas work together to make up the ministry of PCC LEAH and accomplish our vision for homeschooling.

PCC LEAH vision: Support and enable parents following God’s calling to homeschool, to equip their children to be Godly leaders.

With this vision statement in mind, co-op has been set up to meet the following needs:

In the areas of support:

  1. Provides class opportunities for parents that they may be personally uplifted with encouragement (i.e. Homeschool Buzz parent meeting, Mom’s Bible study, prayer or workout).
  2. Provides parents with time to have fellowship and make friends.
  3. Provides fellowship opportunities for students so they may make friends.

In the area of enabling parents:

  1. Offers quality classes that enrich the individual homeschool
  2. Offers classes that meet needs in individual homeschools, (i.e. high school Science labs).
  3. Provides opportunity for a variety of classes all at one place and during one day thus cutting down on the amount of “running” around that a homeschool family may need to do during the week.

In the area of equipping children to be godly leaders:

  1. Gives students an opportunity to be under the leadership of other godly parents (teachers).
  2. Gives students the opportunity to be accountable for homework assigned by someone other than a parent.
  3. Gives a student the opportunity to speak publicly before peers.
  4. Gives the student the opportunity to help work along side parents in maintaining the building.
